Welcome to Your Digital Classroom

This interactive guide is designed for Victoria School Foundation teachers to master the Google Workspace for Education suite. Here you'll find everything you need to set up your accounts, install necessary applications, and begin creating a dynamic digital learning environment. The key to unlocking the full potential of these tools is using your official VSF account.

Always Use Your VSF Account

To access all features, ensure security, and maintain compliance, you must sign in with your official Victoria School Foundation email: yourname@vsf.edu.ph. Personal Gmail accounts do not have the same capabilities or protections.

Accessing Your Account

The most reliable way to use Google Workspace is through a web browser on a computer. This method gives you access to all features and administrative controls.

  1. Open your web browser (e.g., Google Chrome).
  2. Navigate to classroom.google.com.
  3. Enter your full VSF email address (yourname@vsf.edu.ph) and password.
  4. Follow any on-screen prompts to complete the sign-in process.

Installing Apps on Mobile & Tablet

For flexibility, you can install Google apps on your mobile devices. While convenient for quick tasks, some administrative functions, like setting up a grading system, are best done on a computer.

Mastering Google Classroom

Google Classroom is your digital command center. In this section, you'll learn how to create your virtual classroom, invite your students to join, and use the Stream feature to communicate announcements and share resources effectively. These are the foundational skills for managing your online course.

Creating Your First Class

  1. Go to classroom.google.com.
  2. Click the + button in the top-right corner and select 'Create class'.
  3. Enter a Class Name (required), and optionally add a Section, Subject, and Room.
  4. Click 'Create'. Your new classroom is now ready.

Inviting Students

Once your class is created, you need to add your students. Choose the method that works best for you. Remember to tell students they must join using their @vsf.edu.ph account.

Share a direct link with students to join.

  1. Navigate to the 'People' tab in your class.
  2. Click the 'Invite students' icon.
  3. Click 'Copy invite link' and share it with your students.

Send invitations directly to student emails.

  1. In the 'People' tab, click 'Invite students'.
  2. Enter the VSF email addresses of your students.
  3. Click 'Invite'. Students will receive an email to join.

Students can use a code to join your class themselves.

  1. Find the Class Code on your 'Stream' page header.
  2. Share this code with your students.
  3. Students go to Classroom, click '+', choose 'Join class', and enter the code.

Using the Stream

The Stream is your class's announcement board. You can post messages, attach files, and control how students interact.

  • To post, click 'Announce something to your class' on the Stream page.
  • Attach files from your computer, Google Drive items, YouTube videos, or web links.
  • You can 'Post' immediately, 'Schedule' for a future time, or 'Save draft'.
  • Manage student permissions in Settings: you can allow students to post and comment, only comment, or restrict all interactions to only teachers.
  • You can mute individual students or delete inappropriate posts by clicking the three-dot menu on any post.

Communication Tools: Chat & Meet

Effective communication is vital for a successful digital classroom. This section covers Google Chat for instant messaging and collaboration, and Google Meet for live virtual classes. You'll learn how to start conversations, create collaborative spaces, and manage your virtual meetings with essential in-call features.

Google Chat: Instant Communication

Use Google Chat for quick questions, group discussions, and team projects. You can access it via web, a desktop app, or directly within Gmail.

Communication Types:

  • Direct Message: For one-on-one conversations.
  • Group Chat: For small, informal group discussions.
  • Spaces: For long-term projects. Spaces allow file sharing and task assignments, making them ideal for structured collaboration.

Key Features:

  • Start a video call directly from a chat.
  • Share files from Google Drive.
  • Use "@" to mention specific people.
  • Set your availability status.

Google Meet: Virtual Classes

Google Meet is your tool for live video lessons, office hours, and parent-teacher conferences. You can start meetings instantly from Classroom or schedule them in advance using Google Calendar.

Scheduling a Meeting

For planned classes, create an event in Google Calendar and click 'Add Google Meet video conferencing'. This automatically generates a meeting link and adds it to the event details for all invitees.

Essential In-Meeting Features:

  • Mute Participants: Reduce background noise by muting students.
  • Present Your Screen: Share a single tab, a window, or your entire screen to show instructional content.
  • In-Call Chat: Communicate with the class via text during the meeting.
  • Raise Hand: Students can signal they have a question without interrupting.
  • Whiteboard (Jamboard): Launch a collaborative whiteboard for brainstorming.
  • Polls & Q&A: Engage students and check for understanding.

Content & Organization

Organizing your digital materials and schedule is key to a smooth workflow. This section explains how to leverage YouTube for rich educational content and how to use Google Calendar to keep track of deadlines and events. Learn to find and embed videos directly into your assignments and manage your class schedule effectively.

Leveraging YouTube

YouTube is a vast library of educational content. You can embed videos directly into your Classroom assignments to create engaging, multimedia lessons.

How to Embed a Video:

  1. When creating an assignment or announcement, click the 'Add' button.
  2. Select 'YouTube'.
  3. You can either search for a video or paste a direct URL.
  4. Click 'Add video'. You can even add interactive questions to the video for assignments.

Organizing with Google Calendar

Google Calendar integrates with Classroom to help you and your students stay organized. Assignment due dates automatically appear on the calendar.

Classroom Calendar

Shows all assignments and questions with due dates. You can access this from the main menu in Classroom. You cannot add events here manually.

Google Calendar

A full-featured calendar where you can see class due dates and also add your own events (e.g., field trips, exam reminders) or personal reminders (visible only to you).

Help & Support

Even the most tech-savvy teachers need help sometimes. This section outlines the proper procedures for getting technical support and resetting your password. Understanding these processes ensures that technical issues cause minimal disruption to your teaching.

For Password Resets, Contact Your Admin

If you forget your password, you must contact the Victoria School Foundation IT Administrator. For security reasons, teachers cannot reset their own passwords. Your administrator will provide you with a temporary password to regain access.
